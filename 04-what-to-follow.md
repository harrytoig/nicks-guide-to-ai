# What to Follow

*Podcasts, blogs, and X accounts worth your time.*

Pick 2 podcasts, 3 newsletters, and a dozen X follows. Consistency beats breadth. If you try to follow everyone, you follow no one. The goal is calibrated intuition, not winning a Jeopardy category.

---

## Podcasts

### What I actually listen to
- **AI Daily Brief** (Nathaniel Whittemore): 10 minutes a day, daily. Best signal-to-noise for AI news.
- **TBPN**: tech, markets, AI. Go for the shortened versions. The full episodes run long.

### Other stuff worth adding
- **[Dwarkesh Podcast](https://www.dwarkesh.com/podcast)**: long, unhurried interviews with the people actually building frontier AI. Dario, Hassabis, Ilya, Karpathy, Zuck. If you add one long-form AI podcast, make it this.
- **[Hard Fork](https://www.nytimes.com/column/hard-fork)** (Kevin Roose + Casey Newton): NYT tech show. Funny, news-pegged, accessible. Weekly without feeling like homework.
- **[No Priors](https://www.no-priors.com/)** (Sarah Guo + Elad Gil): VC-flavored, technically sharp. Guests run the industry.
- **[Latent Space](https://www.latent.space/podcast)** (swyx + Alessio): the builder's show. Infra, evals, agent frameworks.
- **[Cognitive Revolution](https://www.cognitiverevolution.ai/)** (Nathan Labenz): high-frequency, comprehensive. Good for topic catch-ups.
- **Lex Fridman**: skip most; the Karpathy, Dario, Altman, Hassabis, and Sutskever episodes are foundational.
- **[Training Data](https://www.sequoiacap.com/podcast/)** (Sequoia): short, founder-focused.
- **[Lenny's Podcast](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/podcast)**: PM and product side. Boris Cherny on Claude Code is worth the 90 minutes.

## Reading

Subscribe to 3. Not 10. You are not training to be a librarian.

### What I actually pay for
- **[Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/)** (Ben Thompson, paid): clearest strategic thinking on what AI means for incumbents, platforms, and power. Worth every dollar.

### Other stuff worth your time
- **[Simon Willison's weblog](https://simonwillison.net/)** (free): the single best daily AI writer working. Ships tools, tests every model, links everything that matters. If he posts it, read it.
- **[One Useful Thing](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/)** (Ethan Mollick, free): non-technical, practical, experiment-driven. Written for exactly this moment and exactly your audience.
- **[Import AI](https://importai.substack.com/)** (Jack Clark, Anthropic cofounder, free): weekly. Policy, capability jumps, strategic framing from inside the frontier.
- **[The Batch](https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch/)** (Andrew Ng, free): weekly news roundup.
- **[Every / Chain of Thought](https://every.to/chain-of-thought)** (Dan Shipper, paid): best writing on actually using AI as a professional.

## How I Actually Use This

I do not keep up with everything. Here is the real rhythm:

- **Morning (10 min):** AI Daily Brief on the subway.
- **Weekly:** TBPN shortened versions while I'm doing something else. Stratechery when Ben Thompson ships a big one.
- **When I want depth:** A Dwarkesh episode at 1.5x while cooking or on a run.
- **Ambient:** X feed, tightly curated to the accounts above. Mute generously when it turns into noise.

Consistency beats volume. If you're consuming more than you're building, your ratio is off. Close the tab and go make something.
