# Hitch a Ride

> *"The art of flying lies in learning how to throw yourself at the ground and miss."*
> - Douglas Adams

You are not going to become an AI researcher at Anthropic this year without a PhD. Fine. The interesting news is that the most valuable roles in AI right now are not research roles. They are the people who stand between a research lab and a paying customer, and those jobs are aggressively gettable.

---

## Roles Accessible Without a PhD

### Forward Deployed Engineer / Applied AI Engineer
The highest-leverage role in the ecosystem. You embed with customers, build prototypes against their real data, feed learnings back to product. Anthropic, OpenAI, Harvey, Glean, Palantir. Wants: 3+ years engineering, customer-facing instincts, real LLM production experience. See the [Anthropic FDE job posting](https://job-boards.greenhouse.io/anthropic/jobs/4985877008).

### AI Product Manager
Any company building AI needs one. Single strongest signal: you have launched an AI product. Does not need to be at work. A shipped side project counts.

### AI Outcomes Manager / Solutions Engineer
My job at Glean. You help enterprise customers actually adopt the product. Part consultant, part agent-builder, part therapist. Glean, Sierra, Writer, Decagon, Crescendo.

### Developer Advocate / DevRel
Portfolio-driven. Companies hire from Twitter. If you have 10k followers and ship good demos, you can land one of these without a formal interview process.

### AI Designer / UX
Figma fluency plus AI product intuition. Rare, rapidly becoming more valuable.

### OpenAI Residency (and similar programs)
Explicitly designed for career switchers. $220K. 6 months. No ML PhD required. Apply even if you think you are not qualified.

### AI consultants at Accenture / BCG / Bain
The B-team move, but real. If you already have a consulting background, demand is bottomless.

## The 8 Moves That Actually Work

### 1. Build in public, loudly.
Every project gets a GitHub repo, a 90-second Loom, and a LinkedIn post. The meta-project is documenting the journey. Your visible work becomes the resume.

### 2. Cold outreach means "here is a thing I built with your product."
Generic "I admire your company" emails are dead. Ship a working demo built on the target company's API or platform. Record a 90-second Loom. Send it to a PM or FDE on Twitter or LinkedIn with one specific question. Reply rate is roughly 10x generic outreach. I have personally watched multiple people get jobs this way.

### 3. Pick a vertical and go deep.
Generic "AI enthusiast" has no edge. Legal? Read ten law-firm case studies and follow Harvey closely. Healthcare? Abridge. Finance? Hebbia. Domain expertise plus AI fluency is rarer than either alone.

### 4. Twitter beats LinkedIn for AI hiring.
AI Twitter is where the conversations happen. Follow swyx, Simon Willison, Ethan Mollick, Logan Kilpatrick, Alex Albert. Reply with substance. Share projects. Latent Space's guest list is effectively the AI hiring funnel.

### 5. Warm intros beat applications 20 to 1.
Most AI companies are 50 to 500 people. One good referral from an FDE gets your resume read instead of auto-rejected. Use your Calendly. Ask for 15 minutes. Be specific about what you are building.

### 6. Write.
A Substack with five posts of "here is what I built and what I learned" outperforms a polished resume. Ethan Mollick's entire career pivot was doing exactly this, in public.

### 7. Skip the bootcamps.
Reforge, Refonte, and similar are optional. The only thing they buy you that you cannot get for free is a cohort, which you can replicate with one Discord server.

### 8. Treat the job search as a project itself.
Build an agent that finds roles, drafts tailored cover letters, tracks applications, and pings you when new JDs drop. Show it to the people you interview with. "This is how I ran my job search" is a better answer to "tell me about a project" than any portfolio piece.

## My Own Path, Briefly

Four years at Snowflake in finance and sales. No CS degree. In mid-2025 I started watching Karpathy videos, building agents with Claude, talking to Max Comolli about the technical fundamentals, and writing about what I was learning. Landed a role at Glean as an AIOM in July 2025. Six months later I am building customer agents full-time, writing internal how-to guides, and running customer workshops.

Ramp was about six months from zero to productive. I did nothing unusual. You can do a variation of this, starting today.

## What Not to Waste Time On

- Grinding LeetCode. Unless you are going for a research-engineer role at FAANG.
- Certifications. Nobody cares.
- Reading ten AI books before you ship anything. The books age faster than you read them.
- Waiting to feel ready. You won't.

---

## One Piece of Meta-Advice

The gap between "has shipped one real AI project" and "has shipped zero" is wider than any other credential in the industry right now. Closing that gap is a weekend of work. So go.
