# Ear to the Ground

> *"The mere fact that we live at the bottom of a deep gravity well, on the surface of a gas-covered planet going around a nuclear fireball ninety million miles away, and we think this to be normal, is obviously some indication of how skewed our perspective tends to be."*
> - Douglas Adams

Pick 2 podcasts, 3 newsletters, and a dozen X follows. Consistency beats breadth. If you try to follow everyone, you follow no one. The goal is to develop calibrated intuition, not to win a Jeopardy category.

---

## Podcasts (start with 2)

### Start here

- **[Dwarkesh Podcast](https://www.dwarkesh.com/podcast)**. Long, unhurried interviews with the people actually building frontier AI. Dario Amodei, Demis Hassabis, Ilya Sutskever, Zuck, Karpathy. Dwarkesh out-prepares everyone, which is why you get substance instead of press tour. If you listen to one AI podcast, this is it.
- **[Hard Fork](https://www.nytimes.com/column/hard-fork)**. Kevin Roose and Casey Newton. The NYT tech show. Funny, news-pegged, accessible. The one you'll actually listen to week after week without treating it like homework.

### When you want more

- **[No Priors](https://www.no-priors.com/)** (Sarah Guo and Elad Gil). VC-flavored, technically sharp. Guests run the industry.
- **[Latent Space](https://www.latent.space/podcast)** (swyx and Alessio). The builder's show. Infra, evals, agent frameworks. Stretches you toward technical without snapping.
- **[Cognitive Revolution](https://www.cognitiverevolution.ai/)** (Nathan Labenz). High-frequency, comprehensive. Best for catching up on a specific topic quickly.
- **AI Daily Brief** (Nathaniel Whittemore). 10 minutes a day. My daily driver for staying current.
- **Lex Fridman**: skip most, but the episodes with Karpathy, Altman, Dario, Hassabis, and Sutskever are foundational.
- **[Training Data](https://www.sequoiacap.com/podcast/)** (Sequoia). Short, pointed, founder-focused.
- **[Lenny's Podcast](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/podcast)**. PM and product-side AI guests. Boris Cherny on Claude Code is a highlight.

## Reading (subscribe to 3)

Not ten. Three. You are not training to be a librarian.

- **[Simon Willison's weblog](https://simonwillison.net/)**. The single best daily AI writer working. Ships tools, tests every model, links everything that matters. If he posts it, read it.
- **[One Useful Thing](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/)** (Ethan Mollick). Non-technical, practical, experiment-driven. Written for exactly this moment and exactly your audience.
- **[Import AI](https://importai.substack.com/)** (Jack Clark, Anthropic cofounder). Weekly. Policy, capability jumps, strategic framing from someone inside the frontier.
- **[The Batch](https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch/)** (Andrew Ng, DeepLearning.AI). Weekly news roundup. Good signal-to-noise for a beginner.
- **[Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/)** (Ben Thompson). Paid, worth it. His AI strategy pieces are the clearest thinking on what this means for incumbents, platforms, and power.
- **[Every / Chain of Thought](https://every.to/chain-of-thought)** (Dan Shipper). Paid bundle. Best writing on actually using AI as a professional.

## How I Actually Use This

I do not keep up with everything. Here is the real rhythm:

- **Morning (10 min):** AI Daily Brief on the subway.
- **Weekly (20 min):** Simon Willison's latest + Ethan Mollick's latest. That's it.
- **When I want depth:** One Dwarkesh episode a week. 1.5x speed.
- **Ambient:** X feed, curated to the accounts above. If it turns into noise, mute generously.

Consistency beats volume. If you're consuming more than you're building, your ratio is off. Close the tab and go make something.
